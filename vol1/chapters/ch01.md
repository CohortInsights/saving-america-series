# Chapter 1

## Plot Summary

Kep Westegard, a fiercely competitive thirteen-year-old swimmer, has never willingly missed a day of swim training. This summer, however, he sacrifices time in the pool to attend Forty Liberty, a Revolutionary War reenactment camp in Wisconsin. Kep hopes to win the camp’s $2,000 prize in order to replace money stolen from him during a burglary several months earlier.

Kep attends the camp with his younger brother Max, who earned a scholarship after winning a national history award. The boys join other campers in historically accurate military drills such as marching and drumming. The other two members of the team are TJ, a Black teen with a flair for drama, and Tela, a determined girl who often challenges the roles assigned to her.

During a mock battle exercise, the reenactment becomes chaotic. Smoke, cannon fire, and shouted commands create confusion, and Kep struggles to determine whether the events are part of the simulation or something more dangerous. When soldiers dressed as British redcoats burst from a captured fort, Kep hears gunshots and sees participants falling and calling for help.

In the chaos Kep sees a soldier charging toward Max with a bayonet. Acting instinctively, Kep tackles the attacker to protect his brother, cutting his own hand in the process. The exercise abruptly ends when the staff reveal that the weapons were firing special foam bullets as part of the reenactment.

Kep is reprimanded for dropping the flag during the struggle. As his wound is treated, readers learn that the camp competition will last eleven days and that many campers are motivated by the prize money.

The chapter ends when TJ bursts into the tent to announce that Tela has challenged the boys to a showdown, declaring she is tired of being assigned “girl duties.”

## Character Perspectives

| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Kep  | Determined to win the competition and recover the money stolen from him. | Interprets the reenactment camp primarily as a competition and throws himself into events with intense focus. |

## Reader Revelation Device

**New Clues Introduced**

* Rumors circulate about a “time-traveling army.”
* Max’s father studies theoretical time travel.
* The reenactment exercises are conducted with unusually high realism and discipline.

Although Kep interprets the camp simply as an intense historical competition, these early hints suggest that something more unusual may be connected to the camp. The references to time travel appear only as rumors or background details at this stage, but they quietly foreshadow the deeper premise of the story.

---

## Emerging Questions

* Why was Kep’s home burglarized and his swim-camp savings stolen?
* Are the rumors about time travel merely jokes among reenactors or something more significant?
* What role will the camp’s competition ultimately play in the larger story?
