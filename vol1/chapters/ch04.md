# Chapter 4

## Plot Summary

Sergeant Turner abruptly rushes Kep, Max, and TJ out of the barracks during what appears to be an emergency. TJ interprets the commotion through the lens of the television miniseries he hopes the camp will produce and assumes the chaos is simply another staged event.

Outside, a small plane sweeps overhead and drops objects that produce loud bangs and clouds of smoke but little real destruction. Kep immediately notices the inconsistency: if Fort Liberty is supposed to simulate the eighteenth century, why are airplanes involved?

The boys move to the edge of the camp and meet Querishi, Mule, and Tela. Querishi tells Turner she has been looking for him and directs the group toward a new destination. She announces that they will be heading to the competition early, which excites Kep.

Querishi leads them to the shoreline where Kep had gone swimming the previous night. At a stone doorway built into the rock she explains that the camp’s founder, Annie, is a physicist who built an underground scientific facility beneath the camp. The doorway provides access to a transportation system used by scientists working below Fort Liberty.

An elevator carries the group deep underground. When the doors open, the teens step into an enormous atrium large enough to hold several buildings. Catwalks connect multiple levels, thick cables run along the walls, and giant turbine fans roar throughout the space. At the center of the chamber runs a brightly lit steel tunnel large enough for a train.

Querishi explains that scientists there are developing a high-speed shuttle. Max studies the installation with fascination and asks whether it might actually be a particle accelerator.

Kep overhears a tense exchange between Turner and Querishi about when the teens will be told the truth. When Kep demands an explanation, Querishi initially refuses and says Annie will explain everything in the Situation Room.

Finally she dismisses Turner and Mule and speaks directly to the teens.

There is no competition.

Their training has been preparation for a real mission. Paul Revere and William Dawes have been kidnapped, and the teens must take their place and complete the famous ride warning the colonial militias.

The mission will take place in 1775.

The teens react with disbelief. Max is fascinated by the possibility of witnessing history firsthand. Kep struggles to process the claim of time travel but remains focused on reaching the competition he believes must still exist. Tela immediately refuses to continue and demands to call her parents, but Querishi denies the request.

TJ asks the group to speak privately. Interpreting everything as staged drama, he argues that the time-travel story must be part of an elaborate production for television. He pressures Tela by questioning her commitment to using the prize money to save laboratory monkeys.

Eventually TJ persuades the group to continue. Convinced the story is part of the show, the teens step into the elevator to proceed to the next stage of the journey.

### Character Perspectives
**Kep**  
**Motivation.** Determined to win the prize money and recover what was stolen from him.  
**Understanding of Events.** Confused by the underground facility and the adults’ secrecy but still assumes the competition must somehow continue.

**TJ**  
**Motivation.** Sees the camp as a stage that could lead to a reality TV appearance.  
**Understanding of Events.** Convinced the time-travel story is part of an elaborate production and urges the others to play along.

**Max**  
**Motivation.** Fascinated by science and historical mysteries.  
**Understanding of Events.** Awed by the underground installation and speculates that the machinery may be something like a particle accelerator.

**Tela**  
**Motivation.** Motivated by her activism and the possibility of using prize money to support animal rights causes.  
**Understanding of Events.** Skeptical of the adults’ claims and initially refuses to continue until pressured by TJ.

## Reader Revelation Device

**New Clues Introduced**

* A massive underground scientific facility exists beneath Fort Liberty.
* The facility includes infrastructure large enough for a train-sized shuttle system.
* The adults confirm that the teens have been preparing for a real mission.
* Paul Revere and William Dawes have been kidnapped.
* The teens are expected to replace them and ride in 1775.

For the first time the adults openly present the possibility that time travel is real. However, the chapter preserves uncertainty because TJ interprets the revelation as part of an elaborate staged production, leaving both the characters and the reader unsure how much of the claim should be believed.
