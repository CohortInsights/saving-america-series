# Chapter 5

## Plot Summary

The group is introduced to **Annie**, whose modern and casual style contrasts with Querishi’s more formal presence. The children’s attitudes toward the situation remain largely unchanged: **TJ** searches for hidden cameras, still convinced the situation may be part of a reality television production, while **Kep** impatiently wants the explanation finished so the competition can continue.

Annie begins by repeating Querishi’s central claim: *“America is under threat. We are about to launch your time-travel mission back to 1775.”* The group remains unconvinced, forcing Annie to provide more details. She explains that a man named **Fox** has already traveled back in time and is believed to have captured **Paul Revere** and **William Dawes** in order to sabotage the American Revolution at its beginning.

The children are told the key rules of the mission. Fox’s actions have created a **divergent timeline**, but there is still a window in which the original timeline can be restored before the change becomes permanent. Tela immediately asks the most practical question: **why children are being sent instead of adults**. Annie explains that only individuals with a rare DNA profile can survive the time-travel process, and the children possess that genetic trait. **Max quickly connects this explanation to the earlier thefts from their homes**, realizing that items like toothbrushes were likely taken to obtain DNA samples.

Annie also reveals an important limitation: although the children can survive time travel, they can remain in the past for **only about twenty-four hours**. Adults who attempted to travel without the correct DNA never returned.

Throughout the discussion, TJ remains enthusiastic, interpreting the mission as part of a television production. That belief is shaken when Annie tells him he **cannot participate in the mission** because the **team already operating in colonial Boston believes it would be too dangerous for an African American boy to move freely in that environment**. The historical team is described as strongly opposed to introducing modern complications into the mission.

The conversation turns into an argument and Annie is called away from the room. The chapter ends with TJ proposing a bold idea: **he will secretly sneak onto the time-travel machine and join the mission anyway.**

### Character Perspectives

**Kep**  
**What We Learn.** Kep’s impatience becomes more visible in this chapter. He is focused on getting past the adults’ explanation and returning to the competition.  
**Understanding of Events.** Kep remains skeptical of the time-travel story and still treats the explanation as something that might be part of the camp’s elaborate structure.

**TJ**  
**What We Learn.** TJ’s excitement about the situation is tied to his belief that it may be a reality television production. His confidence is shaken when he is excluded from the mission because of the historical risks he would face in colonial Boston.  
**Understanding of Events.** TJ interprets nearly everything Annie says as part of a staged production, but when he is told he cannot participate, he reacts by planning to secretly board the time-travel machine.

**Max**  
**What We Learn.** Max’s analytical thinking stands out. He is the first to connect the stolen household items to DNA testing for the mission.  
**Understanding of Events.** Max is more willing than the others to consider that the science Annie describes might be real, and he begins piecing together the logic behind the adults’ actions.

**Tela**  
**What We Learn.** Tela continues to act as the group’s most direct questioner. She challenges Annie with the key practical issue of why children are being sent on the mission instead of adults.  
**Understanding of Events.** Tela remains skeptical but listens carefully and presses the adults for clearer explanations.

**Minor Characters**

* **Annie** — A more casual and direct member of the program who explains the details and limitations of the time-travel mission.
* **Fox** — The antagonist who has already traveled back in time and is believed to have disrupted the timeline by capturing Revere and Dawes.
* **The Colonial Team** — A group already operating in 1775 who oppose bringing TJ because they believe it would be too dangerous for him in colonial Boston.

No major historical themes are developed in this chapter. The focus is primarily on explaining the mechanics and limitations of the time-travel mission rather than on historical background.
