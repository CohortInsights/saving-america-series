# Chapter 7

## Plot Summary

The chapter begins with Kep reflecting on TJ’s earlier speculation that Querishi might secretly want TJ to join the mission. Kep doubts the idea but cannot completely dismiss it.

Nearby, Max studies a whiteboard filled with technical diagrams describing the time-travel system. After examining it carefully, Max concludes that the equations resemble a **time-travel algorithm**, similar to ones their father studies, although he notes that the vectors appear different. Kep dismisses Max’s interpretation as dramatic speculation, but Max challenges him and insists the technology might actually be real.

Kep remains focused on what he hopes will be a short mission and a quick return to modern life. A public announcement informs the group that launch will occur in **thirty minutes**. Mule expresses amazement at the scale of the underground facility, and Querishi once again responds cryptically that he will explain everything later. This ambiguity continues to make Kep question whether he understands what is truly happening.

As preparations continue, Tela launches into a criticism of the American government’s record on protecting endangered species. Annie responds sharply, citing **Tela’s application essay**, in which she praised the democratic principle that government derives its authority from the consent of the governed. Tela admits that the statement was actually written by her father’s campaign manager to make the application sound good.

Annie presses the point, reminding the group that people have died to secure the rights they now take for granted. Turner intervenes, arguing that thirteen-year-olds cannot really understand patriotism and should not be expected to grasp the seriousness of the mission. Annie angrily insists that Turner knows how serious the situation is, but Turner replies that the children do not—and that this is why Annie is failing to convince them.

As the launch countdown continues, Kep concentrates on getting Max into the transport vehicle. Mule offers Kep a weapon for the mission, which Kep reluctantly accepts. With only minutes remaining, Max finally boards.

At the last moment, the vent grate suddenly pops open and **TJ emerges**, having successfully hidden inside the transport.

When the countdown reaches zero, Kep feels a crushing wall of force as the vehicle accelerates violently. His vision collapses into tunnel vision as the chapter ends with his thought:

*“We’re going to find out what really, really bad means.”*

### Character Perspectives

**Kep**  
**What We Learn.** Kep becomes increasingly aware that the adults know more about the children than they have revealed, particularly when Annie references information from the application forms.  
**Understanding of Events.** Kep still doubts the reality of the mission but begins to feel the growing tension and seriousness as launch approaches.

**TJ**  
**What We Learn.** TJ successfully executes the risky plan to hide inside the transport, demonstrating determination and improvisation.  
**Understanding of Events.** TJ appears fully committed to joining the mission regardless of the adults’ plans.

**Max**  
**What We Learn.** Max identifies the equations on the whiteboard as resembling a time-travel algorithm, suggesting he may have a deeper scientific understanding than the others.  
**Understanding of Events.** Max increasingly believes the technology may be real and challenges Kep’s skepticism.

**Tela**  
**What We Learn.** Tela’s criticism of the government and her admission about the ghostwritten application reveal a contrast between her public persona and her genuine views.  
**Understanding of Events.** Tela remains skeptical of the adults’ rhetoric but is forced to confront Annie’s argument about the seriousness of democratic principles.
